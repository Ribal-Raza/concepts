Lexical Scopes

Lexical scope determines the accessibility of variables and functions within a nested code structure. The lexical scope is established at the time of code compilation and remains unchanged during runtime. Nested functions have access to variables from their enclosing functions, but not vice versa.

Closures

A closure is a function that remembers and has access to its surrounding scope, even after the outer function has returned. This means that a closure can access and modify variables from its outer scope, even when those variables are no longer in scope within the outer function.

Relationship between Closures and Lexical Scopes

Closures and lexical scopes are closely related concepts. Closures are possible because of lexical scopes. Lexical scopes determine which variables a function has access to, and closures utilize this access to retain references to those variables.
